Scott Weiner, Founder of ClosingBell

Closingbell.co founder Scott Weiner got his creative start at a New York ad agency. Realizing very quickly that digital is here to stay, he learned to code in his spare time and started Brandwagon, a company providing technology and development expertise to startups. Several years later he co-founded his own tech startup that went on to have a successful launch and ongoing profitable operations. Wanting a new challenge and to pursue an idea of his own, Scott founded http://wwww.ClosingBell.co—a source for crowdsourced tips on investing in the stock market.

Q: What is ClosingBell.co, and how did you come up with/what drove you to come up with the idea?

I wanted to create a way for the average investor to discover new investments as well as learn more about what’s going on in the markets and the economy around them in general. Ratings websites are useful for discovering the best places to spend our money. They use the wisdom of crowds to help us make informed decisions, like finding the best places to eat, shop or visit. I wanted to create a service that could help us discover the best companies to invest in, similar in nature to a ratings service, but tailored specifically to the markets. ClosingBell is a transparent, unbiased, democratic platform for market sentiment, where everyone takes full responsibility for what they say.


How does it work, and what value does it provide to users?

There are plenty of financial news sites and opinions, but no one is held accountable for what they say. ClosingBell offers a democratic platform that will ultimately offer the most accurate representation of what the investment community really thinks about equities, and you will know whose advice is credible or not.

The site is free to use, and once you’ve created an account you can start creating stock tips. A stock tip consists of a rating, price target and expiration date. A user can also add rationale or reasons to buy or sell the stock (i.e. #undervalued or #overvalued), as well as a link to a news article or other online reference. Once a user has submitted a few tips, the system ranks them and provides detailed analysis based on the tips they’ve submitted.


Is there a revenue/monetization plan for the site?

A freemium model is currently in place. Users can upgrade their account to access advanced features that give them the ability to find quality tips. We also plan to build a marketplace model to connect the contributors who know what they’re doing to the thousands of people on the platform who don’t contribute. There will also be an API offering for quants to trade on the ClosingBell signals.


What can we expect to see from ClosingBell in the near future? Do you have any long-term plans?

There will be more incentives for people to contribute. ClosingBell thrives on an active community of contributors, and we plan to do a lot of work around rewarding people by giving them access to the advanced features of the platform. There will also be a marketplace-type model at some point. The best people performers on the platform are good at picking stocks for a reason. Whether they’re financial advisors, have industry expertise or own a financial services company—these people provide value, and we want to help them in any way we can. In addition, since data is at the heart of ClosingBell, there will be a lot more work done on analyzing the data to provide better insights for our users on the quality of the tips being posted.


Any advice to first-time entrepreneurs or anyone considering taking the leap out on their own?

You won’t nail your idea the first time around. Be prepared to change your idea several times. You have the ability to mold your idea to fit the needs of your audience, and it will be a constant learning process with your users to get this right.
